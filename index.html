What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science that teaches machines to simulate intelligent behavior. It includes machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and more.

History of AI

Artificial Intelligence has evolved since the 1950s, from puzzle solving programs to deep learning models that listen, write, and even drive vehicles.

Types of AI

AI generally falls into three categories:

  • Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI) – AI designed for a single task.
  • Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) – hypothetically possessing the ability to do any intellectual task a human can.
  • Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI) – a future, surpassingly super intelligent system.

AI in Everyday Life

  • Smart assistants like Siri and Alexa help with daily tasks.
  • Recommendation engines suggest movies, music, and products based on your preferences.
  • Navigation apps use AI for real‑time traffic updates.
  • Healthcare apps detect diseases in medical images more accurately than humans.

AI Myths & Facts

Myth: AI can fully replace human creativity.

Fact: While AI can produce content, the originality and context still come from human guidance.

Myth: More data always means better AI.

Fact: The quality and diversity of data matter far more.

AI Projects You Can Try

  • Build a simple image classifier with TensorFlow.js or Keras.
  • Create a chatbot that answers questions about a hobby or a book.
  • Generate a piece of music using a generative model like Magenta.
  • Build a sentiment analyzer for tweets.

Fun AI Facts

  • The 2016 chess‑winning model AlphaZero beats the world champion in only a few hours of training.
  • Deep‑fake videos are becoming so realistic that regulators are drafting laws to prohibit them.
  • AI-generated artworks have sold for millions of dollars at auction.
  • Machine learning is used to predict the melting of ice sheets and help fight climate change.
